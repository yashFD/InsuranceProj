What is Health Insurance?

Health insurance is an agreement wherein an insurance company agrees to compensate the insured for the medical and surgical expenses incurred during the policy tenure. The medical expenses may incur if the insured falls ill, or meets an accident that leads to hospitalisation of the insured. To be eligible to avail coverage benefits under the policy, the policyholder is required to pay a specific amount periodically, known as premium. Health insurance premium is decided by an insurance company and policyholders are required to pay the same on a monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or yearly basis, without any lapse, to avoid losing the renewal benefits.

Importance of Health Insurance Plans in India

With rising healthcare expenses, access to the required and quality medical treatment can be financially draining. So, to protect yourself and your family against these expenses, you must get everyone insured under a reliable health insurance plan in time. Here are top reasons why you should consider buying health insurance today:

  • To Protect Your Savings

    One hospitalisation has the potential to drain entire savings of lifetime. But if you get hospitalised with a comprehensive health insurance plan, you can save this hard earned money. There are different health insurance plans in the market to cater to different requirements of individuals. But most commonly, they cover the insured for in-patient hospitalisation expenses, pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses, daycare expenses, health check-up, ambulance expenses, domiciliary expenses, organ donor expenses, and so on

  • To Deal With Medical Inflation

    An unforeseen illness can put anyone in stress. And the rising cost of medical treatment in such times only makes the situation worse. And the healthcare expenses are not only limited to hospitalisation expenses, but also include pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses. These include ambulance expenses, expenses related to medicines, diagnostic tests, doctors consultation, room rent, etc. All these expenses can be saved by purchasing an affordable health insurance policy timely.

  • Lifestyle Changes

    Another reason to get health insurance cover is that it helps you fight lifestyle diseases such as cancer, kidney failure, stroke, paralysis, heart diseases, etc. These diseases are most common in people above 45 years of age but due to sedentary lifestyles, stress, pollution, etc. these are now affecting youngsters as well. A comprehensive health insurance plan that covers you for regular medical check-up not only helps you identify the diseases, but also offers financial protection in case of their treatments.

  • To Secure the Health of Your Family Members

    A single health insurance policy can cover your entire family. Many insurance companies offer health insurance plans that allow you to cover oneself, legally wedded spouse, dependent parents, dependent children, dependent parents-in-law, brother, sister, aunt, uncle, brother-in-law, sister-in law, etc. It reduces the hassle of maintaining multiple health insurance plans and keeps you stress free by keeping your entire family protected at all times. Also, purchasing health insurance plans on a family floater basis prove to be more affordable.

  • Timely and Good Quality Medical Treatment

    With a reliable health insurance plan, one can avail of timely and good quality treatment without draining their entire life savings. To avail the treatment, all the insured has to do is visit the nearest network hospital and avail the required treatment as per the terms and conditions mentioned in the policy document. The insured can avail treatment for up to the sum insured, at these hospitals, without paying anything from his/her own pocket. The facility can be availed in case of planned hospitalisation as well as emergency hospitalisation.

Benefits of Health Insurance

Health insurance offers a wide array of benefits to the insured. Following are some of the key benefits of a health insurance plan -

  • Comprehensive Coverage

    A health insurance policy keeps you covered for major healthcare expenses incurred in case of medical emergency. Usually, health insurance plans keep you protected for in-patient hospitalisation expenses pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses, ambulance expenses, domiciliary hospitalisation expenses, daycare procedures, organ donor expenses, treatments taken under AYUSH systems of medicines, etc.

  • Lifetime Renewability

    As per the guidelines issued by Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDA), the insurance companies offering health insurance plans are supposed to offer lifetime renewability benefits to the policyholders. It allows you to renew your health insurance policy without any upper restriction on the maximum age limit. The feature is most beneficial for parents and senior citizens as they can renew their insurance plans without any pressure of looking for new health insurance plans in old age.

  • Cashless Treatment

    Insurance companies have tie-ups with certain hospitals, known as network hospitals, where the insured can avail cashless treatment. If you avail treatment at these hospitals, the insurance company will directly settle the bills at the hospital, and you will not be required to pay anything for the treatment availed, except for non-medical expenses incurred.

  • Portability

    Health insurance portability allows the policyholders to switch from their existing health insurance company to another, in case they are not satisfied with their existing health insurance plan, or find a more suitable plan. Portability feature saves the policyholders from being taken for granted and offers them flexibility to switch in case of dissatisfaction.

  • Tax Benefits Under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act, 1961

    The government of India promotes health insurance by offering tax exemptions of up to Rs. 1.5 Lakh on the premium paid for health insurance plans, under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act, 1961. An individual can also claim a deduction of up to Rs. 25,000 for insurance cover for self, spouse, and dependent children. You can also avail a tax deduction of up to Rs. 25,000/Rs. 50,000 for health insurance plans purchased for parents aged 60 years/ more than 60 years.

Choose a Health Insurance Plan For

  • Family Health Insurance

    A family health insurance policy is meant for the entire family. The policy can cover all the family members under a single sum insured, which is divided among all the members and can be availed by all of them or one member of the family, as per the need arises. Apart from self, a family floater insurance policy can include a legally-wedded spouse, dependent children, parents, parents-in-law, etc., and other relationships, varying from one insurance policy or insurer to another.

  • Senior Citizen Health Insurance

    Senior citizen health insurance plans are designed for senior citizens, parents, or individuals aged more than 60 years to protect them against financial expenses arising because of health issues during old age. Some common coverage benefits offered under senior citizen health insurance plans include expenses related to daycare procedures like chemotherapy, cataract; pre-existing disease cover, cashless hospitalisation, in-patient hospitalisation expenses, pre-hospitalisation expenses, etc.

  • Individual Health Insurance

    Individual health insurance is a type of health insurance plan wherein only an individual, i.e. the policyholder is entitled to receive coverage benefits, in return for the premium that he/she has paid. Under this type of policy, the policyholder is covered on an individual sum insured basis, where he/she can claim up to the sum insured in case of hospitalisation of at least 24 hours. An individual health insurance policy covers the insured for most healthcare needs and is hence, a wise choice.

  • Personal Accident Health Insurance

    Personal Accident health insurance covers the insured in the misfortune event of an accident (rail/road/air) that may be lethal or cause disability or mutilation. These plans not only cover the insured for healthcare expenses, but also for loss of income, in some cases, that occurs due to accidents. Common benefits offered under these plans include accidental death cover, permanent/total disability cover, temporary total disability, hospital cash benefit, transportation of mortal remains, etc.

  • Maternity Health Insurance

    Maternity insurance plans cover all expenses up to a specific predefined limit in case of delivery. These plans protect you for financial expenses incurred due to normal as well as C-section deliveries. Maternity insurance plans offered by some insurance companies also cover you for prenatal and postnatal expenses and newborn baby expenses. Maternity insurance plans usually come with a waiting period, thus you should not delay their purchase if you are planning your family.

  • Children Health Insurance

    As children have weak immune systems, they are more prone to illnesses. Therefore, it is important for them to be covered under reliable health insurance plans. Not many insurance companies offer standalone health insurance plans for children. But they can seek cover under an individual health insurance plan or a family health insurance plan. These plans cover them for major healthcare expenses like hospitalisation expenses, ambulance expenses, daycare treatments, critical illnesses, etc.

  • Newborn Baby Health Insurance

    New born babies require extra care and attention when it comes to their health. Therefore, they need adequate health insurance cover that covers them for basic healthcare expenses. One can add newborn babies on an individual basis or family floater basis, under individual health insurance plans or family floater health insurance plans. These plans cover the newborn babies for hospitalisation expenses, pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses, vaccination expenses, amongst others.

  • Self-Employed Health Insurance

    Health insurance for self-employed professionals offer financial protection in case of accidental emergencies requiring medical treatment and hospitalisation. As self-employed individuals do not work in a public or private sector, therefore they cannot get group health cover. These workers include farmers, retailers, freelancers, small traders, domestic workers, etc. Hence, they need separate cover under individual or family floater health insurance plans.

  • Women Health Insurance

    Women's health insurance plans cater to the varied health insurance needs specifically of women. There are, however, not many health insurance plans designed especially for women but there are many insurance companies to have attempted to create them. These plans can be purchased at affordable health insurance premiums for the sum insured as per the requirement and budget of the potential policy buyer. These plans cover women against several medical expenses including critical illnesses.

  • Health Insurance For Parents

  • Group Health Insurance

    A group health insurance policy is especially designed for a group of employees who work together. Provided to the employees by their employers as a benefit, a group health insurance policy comes at a low premium. Under a group health insurance policy, you get covered for hospitalisation due to an illness, sickness, accident, critical illness, as well as maternity. Note that the employees are covered under a group health insurance policy only until they are working for a particular organisation.

Health Insurance Coverage

  • What is Covered?
  • What is Not Covered?

  • In-Patient Hospitalisation Expenses

    Expenses incurred due to hospitalisation of 24 hours or more, like nursing and boarding expenses, Nurses /Surgeons/ Doctors/Anaesthetists fees, cost of blood, medicines, oxygen, ICU/ICCU expenses, etc.

  • Pre-Hospitalisation And Post-Hospitalisation Expenses

    Expenses arising before and after hospitalisation, for a specific number of days as per the policy terms and conditions. These include expenses related to tests, doctors fee, medical reports, etc.

  • Daycare Procedures

    Treatments that do not require hospitalisation of at least 24 hours, are known as daycare treatments. These include cataract, sinusitis, chemotherapy, radiotherapy, etc.

  • Organ Donor Expenses

    Expenses arising due to harvesting the organ from the donor and for the transplant surgery as covered under the policy. The hospitalisation expenses include room rent, doctors fee, and other expenses.

  • Domiciliary Hospitalisation Expenses

    Treatment taken at home because of unavailability of hospital beds, or when the insured cannot be shifted to hospital due to health conditions. It covers for treatment that exceeds three days, for any injury or illness

  • Road Ambulance Expenses

    Expenses incurred due to transportation of the insured to the nearest hospital in case of a medical emergency are covered up to a specific limit. The ambulance has all the facilities required to transfer the patient.

  • AYUSH Treatments

    It covers in-patient treatments taken under AYUSH systems of medicines which includes Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy. The expenses are covered for up to a specific limit.

  • Cosmetic Treatments

    Plastic surgeries, face lifts, nose jobs, cosmetic implants, botox, etc. are not covered under most health insurance plans.

  • HIV/AIDS

    Most health insurance plans do not cover expenses related to treatments for HIV or AIDS.

  • Self-Inflicted Injuries

    Any injury due caused due to self-harm including suicide or attempt of suicide is not covered.

  • Injuries Due to Alcohol or Drug Abuse

    Injuries due to alcohol or drug abuse, or other addictive substances are not covered.

  • Participation in Adventure Sports

    Any treatment required due to participation in adventure sports like mountain climbing, etc. are not covered.

  • Injuries Due to War, Riots

    Injuries due to war, riots, nuclear contamination, and related perils are not covered.

  • Unproven And Experimental Treatments

    Treatments which are unproven, experimental or done solely for the purpose of investigation.

  • Rest Cure, Rehabilitation, And Respite Care

    Expenses related to admission for enforced bedrest and not for treatment.

Health Insurance Riders

Health insurance riders are extra coverages which help you customise your plan and extend its coverage as per your requirements.

  • Critical Illness Cover

    With this add-on, you become eligible to receive a lump sum amount on diagnosis of serious and long-term illnesses such as cancer, stroke, kidney failure, etc. With the amount received, you can make up for the loss of income resulting due to critical illness, pay for debts, if any, or even avail treatment abroad.Some insurance companies provide cover for critical illness as a part of standalone policy, whereas others offer it as an add-on cover, for up to 50 critical illnesses.

  • Maternity Cover

    Maternity add-on cover can be opted under an individual or family floater health insurance policy. If planning a child, then maternity add-on cover works best for you. It provides financial protection against medical expenses arising due to childbirth. Some insurance companies also provide coverage to newborn babies for several healthcare expenses such as vaccination, etc. the add-on cover comes with a waiting period, which may differ from one insurer to another, but generally ranges between 2 years to 4 years.

  • Hospital Cash

    The add-on cover of hospital cash provides you cover all the non-medical and out of pocket expenses arising during hospitalisation, that are not covered under the health insurance plan. The policyholder gets daily cash allowance as per the limit specified in the policy document, for a particular policy year. Different insurance companies offer different coverage amounts for daily hospital cash benefit, but it generally ranges between Rs. 500 to Rs. 5,000 per day.

  • Room Rent Waiver

    The type of room you choose at the time of hospitalisation, reflect majorly in your hospital bills. A standard health insurance plan comes with a limit on the per day charges of the room. This means that the extra charges incurred due to change in the type of room, lets say from general room to a deluxe room, will have to be borne by the insured. But with the room rent waiver add-on, you can opt for any room of your choice and you will not be charged anything extra, as it provides you for extra cost during your stay.

  • OPD Cover

    OPD (outpatient department) refers to that department of a hospital wherein a patient is required to simply visit it to avail the required treatment and pay the consultation fees to the doctor, instead of getting admitted as a daycare patient. Generally, health insurance plans do not cover you for OPD expenses, but with this add-on cover, you can secure yourself from expenses arising due to the same. The add-on covers you for the cost of medicines, consultation fees, pathological tests, etc

  • Personal Accident Cover

    Considering the rising number of road accidents in India, personal accident add-on cover is a good choice as it offers financial protection in case the insured meets an accident, which results in outcomes such as permanent total disablement, permanent partial disablement, total disablement, or in case of death due to accident. You can opt for this add-on along with a base health insurance policy voluntarily, by paying an extra premium at the time of policy purchase.

  • International Second Opinion Cover

    International second opinion add-on cover provides you the option to consult international doctors before availing the required treatment. You can use this add-on cover in case you are not satisfied with the diagnosis. Choosing this add-on is an empowering method for the insured as they can review the treatment advice and recommendations offered for a particular illness. It will help the insured get insights on additional treatment options and be doubly sure of the treatment they require.

  • Air Ambulance Cover

    This add-on cover helps transfer the sick/injured insured person, to the nearest hospital, in case of domestic as well as international emergency, as per the terms and conditions specified by the insurance company. This add-on cover saves lives in different scenarios like if you require specialised medical treatment during transportation, if you require to be transported in a swift manner on getting injured when visiting a remote area, or require transportation for medical treatment, if cannot be transported via road.

  • Reduction in Pre-Existing Diseases Waiting Period

    Health insurance plans usually come with a waiting period for pre-existing diseases of 48 months. But with add-on cover of reduction in pre-existing waiting period, you can get the waiting period reduced, from lets say 48 months to 24 months. The period of reduction of waiting period may vary from one insurance company to another. You can get this add-on by paying some extra premium. It does require pre-medical check-up though, to be eligible for this add-on cover.

How To File A Health Insurance Claim?

Claim under a health insurance policy can be made in two ways - in a cashless manner and in a reimbursement manner. A cashless claim is when you receive treatment at a network hospital of the insurance provider who is servicing your policy. If the claim request is accepted, the insurance company directly settles the bills at the hospital. Whereas in reimbursement claims, you are required to pay for the hospital bill from your own pocket first, and make a claim request with the insurance company later. The insurance company will process your claim and reimburse you for the same after accepting the claim. You can make a health insurance claim by following the simple steps given below:

  • Step 1: Visit the network hospital or the hospital of your choice and inform the insurance company about the hospitalisation.
  • Step 2: Fill the pre-authorization form at the hospitals TPA (third-party administrator) desk.
  • Step 3: You will receive an approval letter by the claim management team.
  • Step 4: Respond to the query raised by the claim management team, if any.
  • Step 5: Avail the required medical treatment.
  • Step 6: The insurance company will directly settle the bills at the network hospital as per the terms and conditions mentioned in your policy.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

  • 1

    What is a health insurance policy?

    Health insurance policy is an agreement whereby an insurance company agrees to undertake a guarantee to compensate the insured for medical expenses in case of a medical emergency. A health insurance policy protects the insured for several surgical expenses, critical illnesses, and daycare expenses, for a policy term, for up to the sum insured limit. 

  • 2

    Can I purchase more than 1 health insurance plan? How are the claims settled in this case?

    Yes. You can be covered under more than one health insurance plan. In this case, the claims are settled as per the contribution clause, when the claim is higher than the sum insured for one health insurance policy. 

  • 3

    Can I increase the sum insured under my existing health insurance policy?

    Yes. You can increase the sum insured under your existing health insurance policy at the time of renewal, provided the policy comes with greater sum insured options. 

  • 4

    Why should I buy a health insurance policy?

    A health insurance policy not only protects the insured financially for future, but also offers relief in the present. Lifestyle habits such as drinking, smoking, or sedentary lifestyle invite health  issues, which can be minor or serious, may be expensive to treat. To stay financially protected in such times, you need a reliable health insurance plan that covers you at all times. Apart from this, buying a health insurance policy also reduces your overall tax liability by allowing you tax deductions on the premium paid, under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act, 1961.

  • 5

    What are the different types of health insurance plans available in India?

    There are 7 types of health insurance plans available in India. These are individual health insurance plans, family floater health insurance plans, senior citizen health insurance plans, disease-specific health insurance plans, critical illness insurance plans, maternity health insurance plans, and group health insurance plans. At InsuranceDekho, you will find a wide variety of all these plans from popular insurance companies, that serve varied requirements of different customers across India.  

  • 6

    How is health insurance premium determined?

    Different health insurance plans have different premiums. The insurance companies determine premiums after considering various factors that are explained below: 

    1. Type of Insurance Plan: Your health insurance premium is based on the type of plan you choose. If you choose a critical illness insurance plan, then the premium will be high. If you choose an individual health insurance policy, then the premium will be different from that of a family floater plan. To know the difference in health insurance premiums, you can use InsuranceDekho’s health insurance premium calculator.  

    2. Age of the Insured: With age, you become more prone to health issues and are more likely to make health insurance claims. Therefore, you are required to pay higher premiums if you buy health insurance plans in later stages of life. This is why it is recommended to buy health insurance policy when young. 

    3. Policy Term: Most health insurance plans come for a period of 1 year, 2 years, or 3 years. Greater the policy term you choose, the greater will be your coverage, and hence higher will be your health insurance premium, and vice versa. 

    4. Lifestyle Habits: If you drink alcohol or smoke regularly, then you may be denied health insurance completely. However, there are some insurance companies that cover you for the same, for which they charge high premiums. This is because, with these lifestyle habits, you are more prone to health issues.   

    5. Family Medical History: Before deciding your health insurance premium, the insurance company will ask about your family medical history. This is because if someone in your family has a certain disease that you are also vulnerable to, then the insurance company will charge you a higher premium due to increased risk.  

    6. Sum Insured: Health insurance plans come with different sum insured options that you can choose from as per your budget and requirement. The higher the sum insured, the higher the medical coverage and hence higher the health insurance premium. But it is not recommended to compromise on the sum insured to save on premiums. This is because it will not cover you adequately in the time of need.  

  • 7

    Can I cancel my health insurance policy? If yes, will I get a refund for the same?

    Yes. You can cancel your health insurance policy. Every health insurance company provides you a free look period to understand the policy terms and conditions. During this period, if you find any objectionable clause in your health insurance policy, then you can cancel the policy and ask for a refund. Note that you will receive the refund only if no claim has been made during the policy term. The amount of refund will be calculated after taking into consideration the expenses incurred on medical check-ups, stamp duty, and proportionate risk premium, i.e. the number of days you have been covered for risks under the policy. To know more about a premium refunds, it is recommended to go through the policy document.   

  • 8

    How can I renew my health insurance policy?

    It is imperative to renew your health insurance policy to avail continued coverage, and save on accumulated benefits such as waiting period benefits and No Claim Bonus (NCB). You can easily renew your health insurance policy by following the simple steps given below: 

    Step 1: Visit the website of the insurance company and navigate to the ‘Health Insurance Renewal’ page.
    Step 2: Enter the required details such as health insurance policy number and the registered mobile number.
    Step 3: Check the policy details displayed and renewal premium. 
    Step 4: Pay the required health insurance premium via Credit card/Debit card or Net Banking. 

  • 9

    What if my health insurance policy lapses when I am hospitalised?

    There can be two situations. First, where you get hospitalised before the policy expiry date and notify the insurance company before the policy lapses, and the policy lapses when you are still in the hospital. In the second situation, you may be hospitalised during the grace period, the policy lapses then, and you inform the insurance company about the same during the grace period. In both cases, the insurance company will compensate for the coverage availed, as per the terms and conditions stated in the policy document. 

  • 10

    Can I avail coverage benefits if I get hospitalised for less than 24 hours?

    Yes. Most health insurance plans cover you for medical treatments that do not require hospitalisation of at least 24 hours. These are known as daycare procedures. Daycare treatments are performed under local or general anaesthesia in a clinic, hospital, or daycare center. Some daycare treatments that health insurance plans cover you for are chemotherapy, eye surgery, sinusitis, dialysis, angiography, etc.
