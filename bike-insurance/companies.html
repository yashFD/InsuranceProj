How To Choose The Right Two Wheeler Insurance Company In India?

There is no one bike insurance company which is the best for all individuals as the requirement of each individual is different. For some individuals, a bike insurance company which has a wider network of cashless garages is the best, while for others the Two Wheeler insurer which features the highest claim settlement ratio is the best. Thus, there are a number of parameters on the basis of which bike insurance company becomes the best for an individual. Followings are the factors which you must consider when choosing a bike insurance company:

1. Claim Settlement Ratio

The claim settlement ratio is the percentage of claims settled by a Two Wheeler insurance company out of the total number of claims received by it in a financial year. The higher the claim settlement ratio, the better it is. Thus, you should choose a bike insurance company with a good claim settlement ratio.

2. Network of Cashless Garages

Every Two Wheeler insurance company maintains a network of garages at which it allows cashless claim facilities. While choosing a bike insurance company, it is important to check which all garages (especially the ones in your locality) are a part of the insurers network.

3. Availability of Add-on Covers

Two-wheeler Insurance add-on covers are the extra covers which can be opted with own-damage and comprehensive Two Wheeler insurance plans to extend the coverage of a standard bike insurance plan. These add-ons are available against the payment of a nominal premium. You must check if the insurance company chosen by you offers the add-on covers which you want to add to your standard Two Wheeler insurance plan.

4. Customer Support Service

Another important factor to consider while choosing a Two Wheeler insurance company is the availability and quality of the customer support services offered by it. You should choose a company which offers 24*7 customer support. This helps in the easy resolution of your queries and provides you with timely assistance when required.

Types of Two-Wheeler Insurance Plans Offered By Different Insurers

There are 3 types of Bike Insurance plans which are offered by different companies.

1. Third Party Bike Insurance

According to Motor Vehicles Act 1988, this is an essential and mandatory insurance plan. Every bike owner must have a third-party bike insurance plan in order to cover the financial losses incurred due to property damage or injuries, disability, harm caused to another person from their bike. Maximum coverage for property damage is Rs.7.5 lakh. Whereas in case of bodily injuries/death, the liability is unlimited at the part of the insurance company, however, the compensation is decided by a court of law.

2. Standalone Own Damage Cover

This insurance policy covers the losses or damages which are caused to your bike due to accident, natural calamity,fire etc. To get an all-round coverage for your bike, you can opt for the own damage cover along with the third party liability cover. You can also opt for various add-ons along with your own damage cover to customize your insurance policy.

3. Comprehensive Two-Wheeler Cover

Comprehensive bike insurance strengthens the bike insurance coverage as it is a package policy which offers coverage for both third-party liability as well as own damage cover.

Two Wheeler Insurance Companies FAQs

  • Which is the best company for two-wheeler insurance?

    Currently, there are 25 general insurance companies in India that offer insurance plans for two-wheelers. The best two-wheeler insurance company varies from individual to individual depending upon their requirements and budget. However, insurance companies can be compared on the basis of their Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) among other factors. The bike insurance companies which registered the highest motor own-damage claim settlement ratio in FY 2018-19 are IFFCO Tokio General Insurance (96.44%), Royal Sundaram General Insurance (94.07%), and The Oriental Insurance Company (93.16%).

  • Which is the cheapest two-wheeler insurance company in India?

    The premium of a two-wheeler insurance policy depends on a number of factors such as IDV of the bike, type of insurance plan, number of add-ons opted, RTO location, etc. Thus, the cheapest two-wheeler insurance plan varies from bike to bike depending upon the requirements of the bike owner.

  • Can I change my two wheeler insurance policy company?

    Yes, you can switch from one insurance company to another insurance company at the time of renewal of your policy.

  • What are the rules for a bike insurance company in India?

    In India, it is mandatory to have at least a third party bike insurance policy as per the Indian Motor Tariff. The absence of at least a third party bike insurance policy is an offence in the country and attracts a fine of Rs. 2,000 and/or imprisonment of up to 3 months. Also, new bike owners are compulsorily required to have at least a long-term third party bike insurance policy with a validity of 5 years.

  • Will bike insurance companies pay for all the damages in a bike accident?

    The coverage of a bike insurance plan depends on the type of plan purchased and the add-ons opted. Following are the three types of insurance policies available for bikes: Third Party Bike Insurance Policy: Under this, the insurer will only pay for the third party liabilities like bodily injuries, death, permanent/temporary disability, total/partial impairment of a third party due to an accident involving your bike. The third party bike insurance plan also provides coverage of up to Rs. 1 lakh for third party property damage. Standalone Own-Damage Bike Insurance Policy: Under this plan, the insurer provides coverage only for the own-damages incurred by your bike in a road accident, natural calamity, fire, explosion, riot, strikes, theft, or any other mishap. Comprehensive Bike Insurance Policy: This plan provides complete coverage for your bike. Under this plan, the insurance company compensates for own-damages sustained by your bike as well as third party liabilities incurred by it.

  • How does a bike insurance company provide coverage without checking the condition of the bike in India?

    The act of checking the condition of a vehicle for insurance issuance is known as inspection. In the case of two-wheelers, no inspection is required for issuing a two-wheeler insurance policy.

  • What are the losses which are not covered while opting for two-wheeler insurance?

    There are certain losses which are not covered under two-wheeler insurance. These are as follows:

    • Electrical or mechanical breakdown
      Wear and tear of two-wheeler
      Consequential loss/ Indirect loss
      Intentional accidental loss
      Driving under the consumption of alcohol or any intoxicated substance
      Any contractual liability
      Damage caused to/by a person driving any vehicle without holding a valid license

  • 3) What is to be done after I have intimated the insurer of my two-wheeler insurance claim?

    After receiving the intimation, the insurance company will send a surveyor to your place/at the place of the accident to assess the damage incurred. Post the assessment and verification of details, your two-wheeler will get repaired and the claim would be settled.
