About Star Health Insurance

Star Health and Allied Insurance Co. Ltd. is India’s first Standalone Health Insurance provider which commenced its business in 2006. Today, the company has become a market leader which offers multiple insurance products including Health, Personal Accident and Overseas Travel Insurance etc., thereby fulfilling varied needs of different sections of the society. It is also prominently into bancassurance with long standing relationships with several Indian banks. With more than 10,600 employees and 550 branches across the country, Star Health Insurance has become a renowned name in the Indian insurance industry. One of the most promising segments that the company deals in is Health Insurance.

Star Health Insurance offers a wide range of affordable health insurance products for individuals, families, and corporates. The plans cover the insured for medical check-ups, AYUSH systems of medicines, critical illnesses, and hospitalisation expenses. Also, now you can find coronavirus coverage under health insurance plans offered by Star health Insurance. The company also offers health insurance plans for autistic children which makes its offerings unique. One of the major benefits of buying Star Health Insurance plans is that no co-payment is required for individuals below 60 years of age.

What Is A Health Insurance Top-up Plan and Why Should I Buy It?

A health insurance top-up plan is an additional coverage which you can avail if you have an existing plan. These plans reimburse the medical expenditure incurred due to a single illness in excess of the sum insured limit mentioned in your existing policy. Thus, a top-up health insurance plan helps you increase the cover at reasonable premiums.

As a precautionary measure, it is wise to have a top-up plan added to your standard health insurance plan as they offer you the following benefits:

  • Tax benefits under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act, 1961

How to Make A Claim For Star Health Insurance?

Star Health Insurance offers a customer-friendly and hassle-free claim settlement process to ensure timely processing of claims. Under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance. You can make cashless claims as well as reimbursement claims.

What Is the Cashless Claim Process of Star Health Insurance

Follow the steps given below to avail cashless treatment under Star Health Insurance plans:

Step 1: Reach A Network Hospital
Visit the insurance desk at a network hospital. Intimate Star Health Insurance by calling them at 1800 425 2255 / 1800 102 4477 or dropping an email at support@starhealth.in.

Step 2: Show Your Star Health ID Card
Show your Star Health ID card at reception of the network hospital for identification.

Step 3: Submit Required Documents
Submit the documents related to pre-admission investigations and consultation at the hospital.

Step 4: Verification of Documents
Before processing your claim according to the terms and conditions, the doctors will verify all the documents submitted. An assigned representative may visit you at the hospital if necessary.

Step 5: Settlement of Claim
The network hospital will send claim documents to Star Health Insurance after you get discharged. The claim will then be settled directly by the insurer with the hospital.

How To Make A Star Health Reimbursement Claim?

Follow the steps given below to avail reimbursement facility under Star Health Insurance plans:

Step 1: Get Hospitalised
To avail the required medical treatment, you can get hospitalised at a hospital of your choice.

Step 2: Get Treatment and Pay The Bills
After getting the required medical treatment, settle the bills with the hospital. After clearing the bills, initiate a reimbursement claim with Star Health Insurance for the incurred medical expenses subject to the sum insured. Make sure you collect all the medical bills from the hospital.

Documents Required For Star Health Claim Reimbursement

Following documents are required during a Star Health Insurance Reimbursement Claim Settlement process:

• A copy of Star Health ID card
• Duly filled claim form
• Original discharge summary received from the hospital
• Papers related to pre-admission investigations and doctor’s consultation
• Medical reports (X-ray, blood tests, scans, etc.)
• Invoices and prescriptions from the pharmacy
• Receipts from the chemist as well as hospital
• FIR and/or Medico Legal Certificate in case of an accident
• A copy of the KYC documents
• NEFT details, Email ID and contact number

Step 3: Fill The Star Health Claim Form

After initiating a reimbursement claim process, you will be required to fill a Star Health reimbursement claim form. Submit the form along with all the required documents at the nearest Star Health Insurance office or at their online portal.

Step 4: Settlement of Claim

After the completion of all the formalities, Star Health Insurance will settle your claim as per the terms and conditions of your policy and therein mentioned sum insured. Note that you will have to pay for the non-payable expenses from your own pocket.

Star Health Insurance Premium Calculation

The premium amount for different Star Health Insurance plans are calculated on the basis of several factors. Read these factors below in order to make an informed buying decision:

  • Health History: One of the factors affecting the premium amount of your Star Health Insurance plan is your health history. A higher premium is chargeable in case of pre-existing medical conditions. This is because in such cases, the insurance company faces higher chances of obligating a claim in comparison to policyholders who are fit and fine.

  • Number of Members Added Under The Policy: The premium amount for Star Health Insurance plans would cost less if you buy an individual health insurance plan and cover only self. However, if you choose to add your other family members too on a floater basis, then you will have to pay higher premiums as you would then require a higher coverage.
  • Age: As you age, you become more prone to diseases and hence have greater chances of making claims under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance. Therefore, you will have to pay higher premiums if you buy plans during old age as compared to young age.
  • Sum Insured: Higher the sum insured opted for, higher the coverage, and hence, higher the health insurance premium you are required to pay for the health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance and vice versa.
  • Lifestyle: Unhealthy lifestyle habits like smoking, drinking, etc., put an individual at a great risk of getting diseases and hence a greater chance of making claims. Therefore, Star Health Insurance will charge you more premium in case you live an unhealthy lifestyle, and vice versa.

What All Is Covered Under Star Health Insurance Plans?

You can enjoy the following inclusions under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance :

  • In-patient Hospitalisation Expenses: Health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance cover you against in-patient hospitalisation expenses incurred due to hospitalisation because of any illness, sickness, disease, or accident.
  • Pre-hospitalisation And Post-hospitalisation Expenses: Star Health insurance plans cover you against pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses for up to 90 and 180 days respectively.
  • AYUSH Treatments: Star Health insurance plans offer cover for expenses related to AYUSH systems of medicines. These include Ayurveda, Unaani, Siddha, and Homeopathy.
  • Ambulance Cover: Star Health Insurance plans cover you against emergency ambulance charges in case you require urgent medical care.
  • Daycare Procedures: Under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance, you can seek treatment for daycare procedures. These are procedures that require hospitalisation of less than 24 hours. For instance, chemotherapy, radiation, dialysis, and cataract.
  • Organ Donor Expenses: You get covered against organ donor expenses under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance.

What Are The Exclusions of Star Health Insurance Plans?

Health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance come with the following exclusions:

  • Cosmetic Surgery: Treatments related to cosmetic surgery are not covered under health insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance.
  • Self-Inflicted Injuries: Self-inflicted injuries such as injuries resulting from attempt of suicide, are not covered under Star Health Insurance plans.
  • Injuries Due To Participation In Adventure Sports: Expenses arising due to participation in adventure sports activities such as mountain climbing, paragliding etc. are not covered under Star Health Insurance plans.
  • Illness Due To Use Of Intoxicating Substances: Health Insurance plans offered by Star Health Insurance do not cover any treatment required due to use of intoxicating substances. These may include drugs, alcoholism, smoking, and tobacco chewing.
  • Weight Control Treatments: Star Health Insurance plans do not cover you against expenses incurred on weight control procedures including surgical procedures like bariatric surgery or treatment of obesity.

The above inclusions and exclusions may vary from plan to plan offered by Star Health Insurance.
