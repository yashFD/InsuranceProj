About United India Insurance

United India Insurance Company Limited is one of the preferred insurance providers in India which enjoys global recognition and footprint. It was established as an organisation in 1938, managed to nationalise itself as a General Insurance Business in 1972. Later, it got merged with 12 Indian Insurance Companies, 4 Cooperative Insurance Societies, Indian operations of 5 Foreign Insurers, and General Insurance operations of the southern region of Life Insurance Corporation of India.

Today, the company acquires a workforce of 14,000 who work in 2,276 offices and deliver insurance coverage to its 1.74 crore policyholders. The United India Health Insurance offers a wide range of innovative insurance products such as individual plans, family floater plans, senior citizen plans, critical illness plan, top-up plan, and group health insurance plan to meet the healthcare needs of diverse customer segments. Over the years, by providing incredible products and customer service, the insurer has witnessed a staggering growth.

Top United India Health Insurance Plan Types

United India Insurance Company provides coverage to its policyholders against medical expenses which may arise due to the treatment of any injury and/or illness. Here are the top health insurance policies offered by the United India Health Insurance:

1. Family Medicare 2014 Policy - The Family Medicare 2014 is a health insurance plan that offers medical coverage to all the members of a family under a single sum insured. People between the age of 18 years and 80 years are eligible to buy this policy. This plan offers the sum insured amount ranging from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs.10 lakh per family. It comes with an annual policy term and several benefits such as in-patient hospitalisation expenses, daycare procedures, pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation costs, ayurvedic treatment cover, second opinion option and so on.

2. Individual Health Insurance Policy - The Individual Health Insurance Policy offers health coverage on an individual as well as on a family package basis. This policy is divided into three plans such as Platinum plan, Gold Plan and Senior Citizen Plan, where the age of proposer under these plans could be between 18 years and 65 years and sum insured amount ranges from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs. 20 lakh. This policy covers different hospitalisation expenses like in-patient medical expenses, pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation, domiciliary hospitalisation, daycare procedures, ayurvedic treatment and so on.

3. UNI Criticare Policy – The UNI Criticare Policy is a critical illness plan that covers 11 specific critical illnesses such as cancer, coma, heart attack, kidney failure, stroke, etc. As per this policy, if the insured person is diagnosed with one of these critical illnesses, the company shall pay a lump sum amount to the insured person as compensation. People between the age of 21 and 65 years are eligible to take this insurance policy. It comes with the sum insured amount ranging from Rs. 1 lakh to Rs. 10 lakh and lifelong renewability feature. The policy holds a waiting period of 90 days from the date of inception of the policy and a survival period of 30 days from date of diagnosis.

4. Top-Up Medicare Policy – The Top Up Medicare Policy covers hospitalisation expenses up to the chosen sum insured after the threshold level of the policyholder’s policy is crossed. It is a plan that offers you additional protection at an affordable price. However, it is available on an individual as well as on a family floater basis and can be purchased irrespective of whether the policyholder holds any policy at present or not. The proposer of this plan can be between 18 years and 80 years, while its sum insured amount ranges from Rs. 3 lakh to Rs. 15 lakh with the threshold level from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh.

5. Super Top-Up Medicare Policy – The Super Top Up Medicare Policy offers coverage for aggregate hospitalisation expenses incurred due to the hospitalisation of an insured person during the policy period exceeding the sum insured or threshold limit of the policy. With this plan, you can get extra health care protection at a reasonable rate. You can purchase it irrespective of whether you have any health insurance policy or not. The policy is available as an individual as well as family floater basis and can be bought by people between the age of 18 years and 80 years. The sum insured of this plan ranges from Rs. 3 lakh to Rs. 15 lakh that comes with the threshold limit from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh.

What Is A Health Insurance Top-up Plan and Why Should I Buy It?

A top-up health insurance plan is designed to provide extra insurance coverage to your existing health insurance policy. It offers additional support when the sum insured or threshold limit of your present plan gets exhausted during a medical emergency. With this plan, you can get extended coverage support for a single claim. However, Super Top-up is a cover that offers similar coverage but for multiple claims. With this cover, you can get coverage for as many claims as you want in a policy term after your threshold limit is crossed.

United India Insurance offers both types of health insurance plans which are known as Top-Up Medicare Policy and Super Top-Up Medicare Policy. Both the plans can be purchased by any person between 18 years and 80 years of age and come with the sum insured amount ranging from Rs. 3 lakh to Rs. 15 lakh with deductibles from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh. Benefits under the top-up medicare plan are in-patient hospitalisation, pre-hospitalization and post-hospitalisation expenses for 30 days and 60 days respectively, day care procedures, ambulance charges, and so on. Whereas, medical benefits offered under the super top-up medicare plan are in-patient hospitalisation, day care procedures, etc.

United India Health Insurance Claim Process

The health insurance claim refers to the request made by a policyholder with the insurance company for the compensation promised by the latter for medical expenses incurred by the former. A health insurance claim can be processed in two ways namely cashless claim and reimbursement claim.

What is the Cashless Claim Process of United India Health Insurance?

When a policyholder gets the required medical treatment at a hospital which is a part of the network of the insurance company, the claim is processed in a cashless manner, i.e., the insurer pays its liability directly to the hospital.

Here are the steps to raise a cashless claim with United India Insurance Company:

Step 1: Intimate the Insurance Provider
Inform United India Insurance Company or Third Party Administrator (TPA) about your hospitalisation at least 72 hours prior to the admission, in case of planned hospitalisation, and within 24 hours of admission, in case of emergency hospitalisation at 1800-425-333-33.

Step 2: Pre-Authorisation Request
Produce the health insurance ID card issued by the insurance provider or TPA at the helpdesk of the network hospital. Get the cashless request form available with the network hospital and complete it to send it to TPA for authorization.

Step 3: Verification of Documents
The TPA or insurance provider upon getting the cashless request form and related medical information from the policyholder or network provider shall issue a pre-authorization letter to the hospital after thorough verification.

Step 4: Claim Approval
If the TPA or claim settlement team of the insurer requires any other information, they may ask for some other documents too. However, they hold the right to reject the requests too, if they find the documents or details insufficient or false.

Step 5: Settlement of Claim
At the time of discharge, the insured person is required to verify and sign the discharge papers and pay for non-medical and inadmissible expenses. Then, the network hospital sends the final request with the complete hospital bill. After receiving the same, the insurance provider pays off the medical expenses after considering any co-payment, inadmissible expense or any other deduction.

How To Make A United India Health Insurance Reimbursement Claim?

Whenever a policyholder is treated at a non-network hospital of United India Insurance Company, the claim settlement of reimbursement type gets applicable. As per this claim process, the policyholders are required to make the payment of all their medical expenses incurred during hospitalisation and then can raise a claim reimbursement request with the insurer.

Here are the steps to raise a reimbursement claim with United India Insurance Company:

Step 1: Intimate The Insurance Provider
Inform United India Insurance Company about your hospitalisation at least 72 hours prior to getting admitted, in case of planned hospitalisation, and intimate within 24 hours in case of emergency hospitalisation at 1800-425-333-33.

Step 2: Avail Medical Treatment
Get treated at a non-network hospital and pay for the hospital bills. Then, the insured person is required to collect all the relevant medical documents to submit them to the insurance provider or TPA for the reimbursement of insurance claims.

Step 3: Submit Required Documents
Submit the completed and signed claim request form along with the necessary documents to the United India Insurance Company.

Documents required for United India Health Insurance Reimbursement Claim

  • Duly filled claim form
  • Certificate of medical tests, nature of operation performed, etc.
  • Bills and payment receipts, and discharge certificate signed by the doctor
  • Cash-memo from the diagnostic centre, pharmacies, etc.,
  • Payment receipts from doctors, surgeons, and so on.
  • Any other relevant document required by company or TPA
  • First investigation report or post mortem report, if required.

Step 4: Verification of Documents
The TPA or claim settlement team of United India Insurance Company will verify the claim form and medical documents provided. If they find them inadequate, they can ask for some more information or documents or even can reject the requests.

Step 5: Settlement of Claim
If a TPA or insurance provider finds all the information and medical documents right and relevant, they will approve the claim request of the insured person and reimburse all the admissible amount after making relevant deductions, co-payments etc

United India Health Insurance Premium Calculation

The premium amount of the health insurance plans gets affected by some factors. Information of such factors can help you find health insurance policies as per their need and budget. Factors affecting the premium amount of health insurance plans of United India Insurance Company are as follow:

  • Age – Young people are less likely to have health complications than older people, which means insurance providers need to pay compensation more frequently to latter than former. Therefore, policyholders of lower age are charged with less premium and policyholders with more age have to pay a higher premium.
  • Sum Insured – To choose a higher insurance cover, a higher premium is required to be paid. With your selection of a higher sum insured amount, the insurance provider becomes entitled to pay for more compensation during a medical emergency, which impacts the premium amount. Therefore, choose the sum insured amount strictly as needed.
  • Injurious Habits – Habits like smoking, chewing tobacco and other harmful substances on a regular basis increases health risks. The insurance providers see people with such habits as high-risk insurance buyers and charge higher premiums from them than from policyholders with healthy habits. Even, their applications can get rejected sometimes.
  • Occupation – The nature of work of policyholders and the amount of risk involved in it influence the premium charges. For instance, if a policyholder works at a school, he or she might get little or negligible exposure to any health risk, so his or her premium will be normal, but a person who works at a construction site or a factory is more prone to any injury or illness, hence, the premium amount will be higher.
  • Body Mass Index (BMI) – The insurers offer health insurance plans at a higher price to people with higher Body Mass Index (BMI) as they are more vulnerable to health complications like hypertension, diabetes, etc., and they have more chances of rising medical expenses. Hence, they are required to pay a higher premium than people with normal BMI.

What All Is Covered Under United India Health Insurance Policies?

Policyholders can avail the following inclusions under the health insurance plans of United India Insurance Company:

  • In-patient Hospitalisation – Under this, the insurer offers compensation for the medical expenses incurred due to the treatment of a covered illness, injury or any medical condition for which the insured person has to get admitted in a hospital for at least 24 hours. The coverage includes nursing charges, doctor’s fee, oxygen, blood, medicines and drugs, etc.
  • Pre-hospitalisation and Post-hospitalisation – The insurance provider covers the medical expenses of a policyholder incurred during the period up to 30 days prior to hospitalisation and during the period up to 60 days after the discharge from the hospital.
  • Day Care Procedures – It refers to a treatment or procedure which is conducted with local anaesthesia in a hospital or day care centre within 24 hours due to technological advancement and requires no hospitalisation. The insurer covers 140 daycare procedures.
  • Domiciliary Hospitalisation – The insurance provider offers the coverage for medical treatment for at least more than 3 days for an illness, disease or injury, where the patient is treated at home only due to non-availability of room in hospitals or he or she not being in a condition to be moved to a hospital.
  • Ayurvedic Treatment – The insurer covers the medical costs of ayurvedic treatments, but hospitalisation expenses are admissible only when the treatment has been undergone in a Government Hospital or in any Institute recognised by the Government and/or accredited by Quality Council of India/National Accreditation Board on Health.

What All Is Not Covered Under United India Health Insurance Policies?

Policyholders can not avail the following exclusions under the health insurance plans of United India Insurance Company:

  • Vitamins and Tonics – The insurer will not cover medical expenses incurred on vitamins and tonics unless forming part of treatment for injury or diseases as certified by the attending doctor.
  • Alternative Treatments – No coverage is offered for the expenses incurred on alternative treatments such as naturopathy treatment, acupressure, acupuncture, magnetic therapies, experimental and unproven treatments or therapies.
  • War-like Situations – The insurer will not cover the treatment of any injury or illness directly or indirectly caused due to any war, warlike operations, an act of invasion, riots or any act of the foreign enemy.
  • Alcohol or Drug Abuse – The medical expense incurred on the treatment of any injury or illness caused due to the influence of alcohol and/or drug abuse will not be covered by the insurance provider.
  • Cosmetic or Plastic Surgery – The expenses arising due to change of life operation, weight loss procedure, plastic or cosmetic or aesthetic treatment, unless necessitated by accident and prescribed by the doctor.

The above-mentioned inclusions and exclusion may vary from plan to plan offered by United India Health Insurance.
